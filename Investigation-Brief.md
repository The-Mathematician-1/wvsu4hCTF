##### Investigation Brief – Internal Use Only



Using server log analysis and structured interviews with affected university faculty and staff, our Investigation Portal has identified several potential vulnerabilities within the campus network. These vulnerabilities may have allowed an unauthorized user (threat actor) to gain access to internal systems.



Preliminary findings suggest that weak authentication practices, exposed information within web applications, and improperly secured files may have contributed to the breach. In several cases, sensitive data appears to have been stored or transmitted without adequate protection, making it easier for the attacker to discover and exploit.



At this time, it is believed that the attacker moved through the network by locating hidden information, decoding encrypted messages, and taking advantage of misconfigured system settings. These actions indicate a methodical approach and a strong understanding of basic cybersecurity weaknesses.



Your task as junior cybersecurity analysts is to examine the available evidence, identify how these vulnerabilities were exploited, and recover any hidden or encoded information left behind by the attacker.
